History

The history of Surkhet dates back to ancient times, and the region is believed to have been inhabited since prehistoric times. The district was originally a part of the Khasa Kingdom, which was established in the 8th century. The district was ruled by the Malla Kingdom in the 15th century, and Surkhet was one of the principalities under the Karnali Zone. The kingdom was later annexed by the Gorkha Kingdom in the late 18th century, and Surkhet became a part of Nepal. During the Nepalese Civil War (1996-2006), Surkhet was a center of conflict between the Maoist rebels and the government forces. The conflict resulted in the loss of many lives and the displacement of many people in the district.
