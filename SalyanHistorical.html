Historical Sites of Salyan

Khalanga Fort

Khalanga Fort is a historical fortress located in the district headquarters of Salyan. The fortress is believed to have been built in the 16th century and served as a strategic military outpost for the rulers of the Chaubise Rajya. The fortress is situated on a hill overlooking the town of Khalanga and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can explore the fortress and its various chambers, including the watchtower and the prison cell.

Shiva Mandir

Shiva Mandir is a Hindu temple located in the town of Salyan. The temple is believed to have been built in the 18th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism. The temple is a popular destination for Hindu pilgrims from all over Nepal and India, who come to offer prayers and offerings to Lord Shiva. Visitors can explore the temple complex and its various shrines, including the main temple dedicated to Lord Shiva

Rani Mahal

Rani Mahal is a historical palace located in the town of Salyan. The palace is believed to have been built in the 19th century by the queen of Salyan, who was known for her beauty and intelligence. The palace is famous for its unique architecture, which is characterized by its red-brick walls and ornate windows. Visitors can explore the palace and its various chambers, including the throne room and the royal bedroom. The palace also offers a unique opportunity to learn about the history and culture of the Salyan district.

Kapurkot Durbar

Kapurkot Durbar is a historical palace located in the town of Kapurkot in Salyan. The palace is believed to have been built in the 18th century and served as the residence of the rulers of the Kapurkot Kingdom. The palace is famous for its unique architecture, which is characterized by its intricate wood carvings and ornate windows. Visitors can explore the palace and its various chambers, including the throne room and the royal bedroom.
