Historical Sites of Mugu

Rara Lake

Rara Lake is a high-altitude lake located in the Rara National Park in Mugu. It is the largest lake in Nepal and is surrounded by beautiful forests and mountains. The lake is believed to have been formed by a landslide in the 10th century and is considered a sacred site by the local people. Visitors can trek to the lake and enjoy its natural beauty and tranquility.

Chankheli Lhakhang

Chankheli Lhakhang is a Buddhist temple located in the village of Chankheli in Mugu. The temple is believed to have been built in the 14th century and is dedicated to the Buddhist deity Tara. Visitors can explore the temple and learn about its historical and religious significance.

Khani Lhakhang

Khani Lhakhang is a historical temple located in the village of Khani in Mugu. The temple is believed to have been built in the 14th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism. Visitors can explore the temple and learn about its historical and religious significance

Mugum Karmarong Chorten

: Mugum Karmarong Chorten is a Buddhist stupa located in the town of Gamgadhi in Mugu. The stupa is believed to have been built in the 14th century and is considered a sacred site by the local people. Visitors can observe the stupa and learn about its historical and religious significance.
