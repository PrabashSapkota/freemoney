Master Bhavani Temple

Master Bhavani Temple is a Hindu temple located in Dolpa, a remote district in western Nepal. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Bhavani, who is considered a manifestation of the Hindu goddess Durga. The temple is situated in the village of Tripurakot in Dolpa and is believed to have been built in the 12th century. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus from Nepal and India, and is especially important for the people of Dolpa. According to local legend, the temple was built by a group of Pandits who were fleeing persecution in India. They brought with them a statue of the goddess Bhavani, which they placed in the temple. The statue is said to be incredibly powerful, and is believed to have the ability to grant wishes to those who pray to it. The temple is located in a scenic area surrounded by mountains, and is accessible by road or by trekking. It is a popular destination for tourists interested in exploring the culture and traditions of Nepal.

Dhakar Gumba

Dhakar Gumba, also known as Dhechak Monastery, is a Buddhist monastery located in Dolpa, a remote district in western Nepal. The monastery is situated in the village of Dho Tarap, which is one of the highest inhabited villages in the world at an altitude of 4,080 meters above sea level. Dhakar Gumba is one of the oldest monasteries in Dolpa, believed to have been founded more than 800 years ago by a Tibetan Buddhist scholar named Rinchen Zangpo. The monastery has a unique architecture, with its buildings constructed into the side of a cliff, and is considered a sacred site by the people of Dolpa. The monastery is home to about 200 monks and is renowned for its rich collection of ancient Buddhist scriptures, paintings, and artifacts. Visitors can observe the daily life of the monks, attend prayer ceremonies, and explore the monastery's various shrines and meditation halls. The surrounding area of Dhakar Gumba is also a popular destination for trekking and hiking, offering stunning views of the Himalayan mountains and opportunities to experience the traditional culture and lifestyle of the Dolpa people.
