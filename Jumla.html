Introduction

Jumla district is one of the seventy-seven districts of Nepal located in the Karnali Province. The district covers an area of 2,531 km² and has a population of 108,921 according to the 2011 Nepal census, making it one of the smallest and least populous districts in the country. Jumla is home to various ethnic groups, including Chhetri, Kami, Magar, Dalit, and Thakuri. The district is divided into seven local units and has Jumla Khalanga as its district headquarters. Jumla is known for its natural beauty and breathtaking landscapes, including the Sisne Himal mountain range and the Rara Lake, which is the largest lake in Nepal. The district is also home to several rivers and waterfalls, including the Tila River and the Mahabharat waterfall.

History

The history of Jumla dates back to ancient times, and the region is believed to have been inhabited since the Stone Age. Archaeological evidence suggests that Jumla was once part of the Khasa Kingdom, which was established in the 8th century. The district was originally a part of the Malla Kingdom, which was divided into several principalities in the 15th century. Jumla was one of these principalities, ruled by the Sen Dynasty. The kingdom was later annexed by the Gorkha Kingdom in the late 18th century, and Jumla became a part of Nepal. During the Nepalese Civil War (1996-2006), Jumla was a stronghold of the Communist Party of Nepal (Maoist). The conflict resulted in the loss of many lives and the displacement of many people in the district.

Jumla district is situated in the western part of Nepal, in the Karnali Province. It is surrounded by several districts, including Mugu to the north, Dolpa to the east, Kalikot to the south, and Humla to the west. The district covers an area of 2,531 km² and is predominantly mountainous, with elevations ranging from 1,370 meters to over 7,000 meters. The district is home to several mountain ranges, including the Saipal Himal range, which has several peaks over 7,000 meters, and the Kanjiroba Himal range.
