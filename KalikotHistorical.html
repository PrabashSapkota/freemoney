Historical Sites of Kalikot

Khandeshwori Temple

Khandeshwori Temple is a Hindu temple located in the town of Manma in Kalikot. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Khandeshwori, who is believed to protect the local people from evil spirits and diseases. The temple is known for its unique architecture, which features a multi-tiered roof and intricate carvings on the walls.

Nagma Temple

Nagma Temple is another Hindu temple located in the town of Manma in Kalikot. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Nagma, who is believed to bring prosperity and good fortune to the local people. The temple is known for its elaborate rituals and festivals, which attract devotees from all over Nepal and India.

Khasa Fort:

Khasa Fort is a historical fortress located in the village of Khasa in Kalikot. The fortress was built in the 16th century by the local Khasa rulers and served as a strategic military outpost. The fortress is known for its unique architecture, which features a complex system of walls and watchtowers.

Sipkhana:

Sipkhana is a historical site located in the village of Khandachakra in Kalikot. The site is believed to have been a center of ancient trade and commerce, and is known for its well-preserved stone carvings and inscriptions. The site also features several ancient temples and shrines.
