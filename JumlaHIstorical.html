Historical Sites of Jumla

Chandannath Temple

Chandannath Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in the center of Jumla town. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is considered one of the most important religious sites in the region. The temple is famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Nepali month of Bhadra and attracts thousands of visitors from all over Nepal.

Kanak Sundari Temple:

Kanak Sundari Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in the village of Kanak Sundari in Jumla district. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Kanak Sundari, who is believed to be an incarnation of the goddess Parvati. The temple is a popular destination for Hindu pilgrims from all over Nepal.

Ghumurtal:

Ghumurtal is a historical lake located in the eastern part of Jumla district. The lake is believed to have been created by the Pandavas during their exile, and is considered a sacred site by Hindus. The lake is also a popular destination for trekking and hiking, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountain landscapes.

Jumla Palace

Jumla Palace is a historical palace located in the center of Jumla town. The palace was the residence of the Khasa kings during the 12th century and is considered one of the most important historical sites in the region. The palace is now in ruins, but its remains are still visible and attract visitors from all over Nepal.
