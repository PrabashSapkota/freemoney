Historical Sites of Rukum

Rukumkot Palace:

Rukumkot Palace is a historical palace located in the Rukumkot village of Rukum. It is believed to have been built in the 16th century and served as the residence of the local rulers. The palace has a unique architectural design and is built on a hill overlooking the surrounding area. Visitors can explore the palace and its various chambers, which include the throne room, the guest rooms, and the prison cells.

Chankheli Lek:

Chankheli Lek is a historical site located in the Chankheli village of Rukum. It is an ancient stone inscription that dates back to the 6th century AD and is considered one of the oldest inscriptions in Nepal. The inscription contains information about the rulers of the Licchavi dynasty and their achievements.

Thabang Palace:

Thabang Palace is a historical palace located in the Thabang village of Rukum. It is believed to have been built in the 18th century and served as the residence of the local rulers. The palace has a unique architectural design and is built on a hill overlooking the surrounding area. Visitors can explore the palace and its various chambers, which include the throne room, the guest rooms, and the prayer halls

Sisne Temple:

Sisne Temple is a Hindu temple located in the Sisne village of Rukum. It is believed to have been built in the 13th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism. The temple is famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Nepali month of Bhadra (August-September) and attracts thousands of visitors. During the festival, the locals perform traditional dances and offer prayers and offerings to Lord Shiva.
