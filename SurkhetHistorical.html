Historical Sites of Surkhet

Bheri River

Bheri River is a historic river that flows through the district of Surkhet. It is considered one of the largest and most important rivers in Nepal and has played a significant role in the history and culture of the region. The river is also known for its scenic beauty and is a popular destination for rafting and other water sports.

Kakre Bihar

Kakre Bihar is a Buddhist monastery located in the village of Kakre in Surkhet district. The monastery is believed to have been founded in the 10th century and is considered one of the oldest and most important Buddhist monasteries in the region. The monastery is known for its unique architecture and is home to several ancient Buddhist artifacts and scriptures.

Ghatgaun Palace:

Ghatgaun Palace is a historic palace located in the town of Birendranagar in Surkhet district. The palace was built in the mid-19th century and served as the residence of the ruling Shah dynasty of Nepal. The palace is renowned for its unique architecture, with its buildings constructed in a traditional Nepalese style.

Lekh Nath Temple

Lekh Nath Temple is a historic temple located in the town of Birendranagar in Surkhet district. The temple is believed to have been built in the 17th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its unique architecture and is a popular destination for Hindu pilgrims from all over Nepal.
