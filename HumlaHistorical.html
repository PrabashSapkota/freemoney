Historical Sites of Humla

Raling Gompa

Raling Gompa is a 400-year-old Buddhist monastery located in the Limi Valley of Humla, a remote district in the northwest corner of Nepal. The monastery is considered one of the oldest and most important monasteries in the region, with a rich collection of ancient Buddhist scriptures, paintings, and artifacts. Raling Gompa is situated in a scenic area surrounded by snow-capped mountains, and is accessible by trekking from the nearby town of Simikot. The monastery is home to about 60 monks and is renowned for its unique architecture, with its buildings constructed into the side of a cliff. The monastery is also famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Tibetan month of June and attracts thousands of visitors from all over Nepal and Tibet. During the festival, the monks perform traditional masked dances and chant prayers, and devotees offer donations and prayers to the Buddha and various deities. Visitors to Raling Gompa can observe the daily life of the monks, attend prayer ceremonies, and explore the monastery's various shrines and meditation halls. The monastery also offers a unique opportunity to experience the traditional culture and lifestyle of the Limi Valley and the Humla region.

Namkha Khyung Dzong

Namkha Khyung Dzong is a historical fortress located in the village of Muchu in Humla, a remote district in the northwest corner of Nepal. The fortress is believed to have been built in the 14th century and served as a strategic military outpost for the rulers of the Malla dynasty. The fortress is situated on a hill overlooking the village of Muchu and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountain landscapes. It is also home to several ancient shrines and artifacts, including a sacred stone pillar and a statue of the deity Dorje Phurba. Visitors to Namkha Khyung Dzong can explore the fortress and its various chambers, including the prison cell and the watchtower. The fortress is also a popular destination for trekking and hiking, offering a unique opportunity to experience the traditional culture and lifestyle of the people of Muchu and the Humla region. In addition to its historical and cultural significance, Namkha Khyung Dzong is also famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Tibetan month of August and attracts pilgrims and visitors from all over the region. During the festival, the locals perform traditional masked dances and offer prayers and offerings to the deities.

Kharpunath Temple

Kharpunath Temple is a Hindu temple located in the village of Kharpunath in Humla, a remote district in the northwest corner of Nepal. The temple is believed to have been built in the 11th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism. Kharpunath Temple is situated in a scenic area surrounded by snow-capped mountains, and is accessible by trekking from the nearby town of Simikot. The temple is a popular destination for Hindu pilgrims from all over Nepal and India, who come to offer prayers and offerings to Lord Shiva. The temple is famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Nepali month of Bhadra (August-September) and attracts thousands of visitors. During the festival, the locals perform traditional dances and offer prayers and offerings to Lord Shiva and the goddess Parvati. Visitors to Kharpunath Temple can explore the temple complex and its various shrines, including the main temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, as well as the shrines of other Hindu deities such as Ganesh and Saraswati. The temple also offers a unique opportunity to experience the traditional culture and lifestyle of the people of Kharpunath and the Humla region.

Tashigang Gompa

Tashigang Gompa is a Buddhist monastery located in the Limi Valley of Humla, a remote district in the northwest corner of Nepal. The monastery is situated at an altitude of over 4,000 meters and is considered one of the highest monasteries in the world. Tashigang Gompa is believed to have been founded in the 13th century by the great Tibetan yogi and saint, Milarepa. The monastery is renowned for its unique architecture, which is characterized by its white-washed walls and red-painted windows. The monastery is home to about 30 monks and is famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Tibetan month of August and attracts thousands of visitors from all over Nepal and Tibet. During the festival, the monks perform traditional masked dances and chant prayers, and devotees offer donations and prayers to the Buddha and various deities. Visitors to Tashigang Gompa can observe the daily life of the monks, attend prayer ceremonies, and explore the monastery's various shrines and meditation halls.
