Introduction

Humla District is a district in the Karnali Province of Nepal. It covers an area of 5,655 square kilometres (2,183 sq mi) and has a population of 50,858 according to the 2011 Nepal census. Humla is situated in the remote Himalayas, and is one of the least developed districts of Nepal. The district is home to various ethnic groups, including the Khasa, Sherpa, and Tibetans. Simikot is the district headquarters and largest town in Humla.

History

The history of Humla dates back to ancient times, and is believed to have been inhabited by various ethnic groups over the centuries. The district was formerly part of the Khasa kingdom, which was established during the 8th century. The Khasa kingdom expanded to a great extent in the 13th and 14th centuries, but collapsed in the late 14th century and was divided into smaller principalities. Before the unification of modern Nepal, Humla was part of the Baise Rajya (22 principality confederacy). The principality was sovereign but intermittently allied with other principalities until they were annexed during the unification of modern Nepal from 1744 to 1810.

Humla is situated in the remote Himalayas and is one of the least developed districts of Nepal.
