Historical Sites of Jajarkot

Sinja Valley

Sinja Valley is a historical site located in Jajarkot district, Nepal. The valley is home to the ruins of the ancient capital of the Khasa Kingdom, which dates back to the 12th century. The valley is also known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional way of life, with the local inhabitants still practicing ancient rituals and customs. The valley has several temples, including the Chandannath Temple, which is considered one of the most important temples in the region. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Nepali month of Baisakh (April-May). During the festival, the locals perform traditional dances and offer prayers and offerings to Lord Shiva.

Khalanga Fort

Khalanga Fort is a historical site located in the center of Jajarkot district. The fort is believed to have been built in the 12th century by the Khasa Kingdom as a strategic military outpost. The fort is situated on a hill overlooking the town of Khalanga and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountain landscapes. The fort is also home to several ancient shrines and artifacts, including a stone inscription dating back to the 14th century. Visitors to Khalanga Fort can explore the fort and its various chambers, including the watchtower and the prison cell.

Badimalika Temple

Badimalika Temple is a historical and religious site located in Jajarkot district. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Bhagwati and is one of the most important temples in the region. The temple is situated at an altitude of over 4,000 meters and is accessible by trekking from the nearby town of Khagenkot. The temple is famous for its annual festival, which takes place in the Nepali month of Bhadra (August-September) and attracts thousands of pilgrims from all over Nepal and India. During the festival, the locals perform traditional dances and offer prayers and offerings to the goddess Bhagwati. Visitors to Badimalika Temple can explore the temple complex and its various shrines, as well as witness the traditional culture and lifestyle of the people of Jajarkot.
